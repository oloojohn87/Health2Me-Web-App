Information We Collect

Personally Identifiable User Information

Our site collects the information required when you sign up for our services, as well as information that users voluntarily provide or have provided on their behalf, including personal and confidential healthcare or other information about a user (“User Information”), that we may associate with a specific account. Examples of User Information include but are not limited to basic identification and demographic information (such as name, birthdate, and gender), contact information (such as phone number, mobile phone number (if provided), and email or physical addresses), unique identifiers (such as username, password or identification number), health records (including but not limited to medical reports, images and audiovisual materials and test results), insurance information, financial or payment information, other personal and confidential information, user contacts and your activities and interactions with Health2.me. We may receive information about you from medical professionals and other users, such as when they make a referral or submit a medical record about you. When you post content on Health2.me, we may also extract, aggregate and analyze additional data related to such content (metadata), such as the name of the patient or medical professional, and time, date and place with respect to the record.

We collect information from and about the devices you use to access Health2.me. This includes such things as your IP address (which is also used to help verify your identity and to diagnose problems with our servers and to administer our website) and identifiers associated with your devices.

If you are on our mobile website or accessing the site through our mobile application, you may be asked whether you want us to access your geographic location based on the location of your mobile device. If you select ‘yes’ and save that setting, we may access and collect that data and use it in accordance with this Privacy Policy.

To the extent any information collected on this website constitutes Protected Health Information, then our HIPAA and HITECH compliant privacy practices will apply. To the extent those laws are applicable to any information collected via Health2.me, our policies developed under those additional laws will apply to that information.

Non-Personally Identifiable User Information

Cookies Health2.me uses “cookies.” Cookies are text files that may be stored in your computer’s browser or on the user’s hard drive by the software we use on our network of sites to store your preferences and perform other administrative functions. We use cookies to understand site usage and to improve the user experience, create personalized offerings, and improve the content and offerings on our network of sites. Some of our software vendors use cookies for purposes that may be different from those stated in our Privacy Statement, and our Privacy Statement does not cover our software vendors’ collection and use of your information by way of cookies in connection with our network of sites. Sensitive information is stored long-term only on Health2.me’s servers.

Web Beacons “Web beacons” refer to Internet tools, such as transparent images on the website or in emails that we may send to you that help us to determine, for instance, whether a page has been viewed or an email opened. For example, when you ask us to send you information, we may use web beacons to determine how many of the emails we sent you were actually opened. In general, any electronic image viewed as part of a web page, including a banner ad, can contain a web beacon.

Other Information We may collection information regarding the type of browser and device you use as well as aggregate and de-identified data regarding users of our site. We may also collect other information such as what websites are referring traffic or linking to our website.

We may ask that you participate in anonymous surveys, which allow us to collect additional data to help us improve our site. Participation is voluntary and is not connected to any of your personal information.

Because non-personal information does not identify who you are, we do not limit the ways we may use or share non-personal information. For example, we may share non-personal information with our affiliates, employees and agents, other businesses, and the government.
